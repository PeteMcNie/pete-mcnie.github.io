Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

The main overriding principle of neuroplasticity is that when we learn we are actually creating new pathways in our brain. This ability to create new pathways does not end when we leave childhood, as previously believed, but the brain continues to adapt and develop throughout our lives.

Knowing that the brain has this neuroplasticity enables us to gain a growth mindset. This growth mindset is the knowledge that we actually get smarter or more skilled by a sustained effort at trying to learn something.

By understanding the principles of neuroplasticity we know that we can influence our brains' ‘growth’ and how it learns. This means that we know that if we keep searching for answers and struggling through the hard bit of learning, we know we are actually strengthening our brains’ pathways and are more likely to keep trying to learn when the going gets tough instead of giving up.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity.

A few methods of learning have been shown to enhance our brains’ neuroplasticity are:

  • Traveling
  • Learning a musical instrument
  • Non-dominant hand exercises
  • Reading fiction
  • Expanding your vocabulary
  • Sleeping

Every time we carry out a repetition of these activities, and more, we reinforce the pathway in our brain which in turn changes the way our brain works. As these pathways grow what is at first, a struggle, becomes easier as the pathway to that area in the brain is stronger. This leads us from the beginnings of learning a skill, by forming the pathways for the first time, to the automatic flow of thinking using strong established pathways in the brain.

The Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

The growth mindset is us personally understanding that we can get smarter and more talented through the time and effort we put in. It is the opposite from the fixed mindset which tells us that it does not matter how much effort we put in we will never be smart or talented enough.

Knowing and believing that you growth mindset will lead you to approach learning with completely different behaviour. You will put more effort into learning because you know that at some point you will figure your problem out. You will end up putting more time and effort in to find the answer and end up strengthening those internal brain pathways far more than someone who gives up straight away.

 With a growth mindset, individuals may achieve more than others because they are worrying less about seeming smart or talented and putting more of their energy into learning.
